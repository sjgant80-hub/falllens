FallLens

A sovereign browsing primitive. Type a URL. A peer in your trusted network fetches it, signs the bytes with their Ed25519 key, and streams them back to you. Your local page verifies the CID, then renders inside a sandboxed iframe.

Why

Sometimes your direct network can't reach a URL — blocked at the coffee shop, throttled, geo-region mismatch, or your friend just has a faster pipe. FallLens lets your peers help each other read the open web. When your network can't, a friend's can.

The primitives

  • FallLink — WebRTC data-channel peer transport
  • FallStore — content-addressed local cache (CID = SHA-256 of bytes)
  • FallSignature — Ed25519 peer signatures on every relay bundle

The trust model

Peers are not trusted with content authenticity — only with delivery. Every response bundle includes the peer's DID + signature + CID. Your local page verifies the CID before rendering. If the relayer swaps the bytes, the CID won't match, and the render is aborted.

Not a circumvention tool

FallLens is a general peer-assisted browsing primitive. Relayers set their own blocklist. Rate limits and size caps protect relays from abuse. Users are responsible for what they fetch. Content is rendered in a strictly sandboxed iframe.

A lens, not a browser. It sees what a friend sees.
